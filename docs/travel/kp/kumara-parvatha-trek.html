Kumara Parvatha Trek

I had not trekked for a few months and was badly missing the jungles. So on the weekend of 7-9 Nov 2025, I decided to go on a trek to Kumara Parvatha. Kumara Parvatha, also referred to as KP is widely known as the most difficult trek in Karnataka. While this fame is associated with the route from Kukke, the route we took was a bit different.

Routes to the peak

KP can be reached by 2 routes. One of the routes starts from Kukke town right behind the famous Kukke Subramanya temple. This trail is difficult and covers a distance of 14 kms one way. Most of it is steep climb over rocky sections. This is the route which earns this trek the title of the most difficult trek of South India. I had trekked via this path thrice earlier.

The other route starts from Bidehalli forest checkpost in Somwarapete. I had never been from this side. This trail is relatively easier compared to the Kukke one and one has to cover just 7km to reach the peak. The trail passes through forests with mostly flat or gradual climb. There are a couple of rocky patches that are steep.

I went on this trek with Plan The Unplanned (PTU). We did a circuit trek starting from Bidehalli and ending at Kukke. I generally do not capture photos while on the trail. But on this trek, for a change, I captured a few pics and I will be putting some of them here.

The Journey and the trek

We started our journey from Bangalore on the night of Friday. We reached our homestay early on Saturday. We got a bit delayed in freshening up and having breakfast and I don't really remember when we left from our homestay. We got in the bus and the bus drove us up to the beginning of the trailhead. From here, we had to walk another km to reach Bidehalli forest office. Most of this walk was on flat ground with a bit of incline up and down.

We got our permits and bags checked and started the trek. We were a group of 20 trekkers accompanied by 2 trek leads and 2 local guides. The trail starting from the forest office crosses over a wide stream which is spanned by a hanging bridge. The bridge shakes while one walks on it, adding another sense of adventure to the experience.

The trail is like any other trail in the western ghats and I quickly felt at home within the dense forest.

We kept walking inside the forest for quite some time. Finally we emerged from the forest to be greeted with the first view of nearby valley and peaks. The weather wasn't too good and the hazy conditions did not let us get a good view of the peaks. Since other people from our group were far behind, I spent a few minutes sitting here and enjoying the view.

The trail entered forest again and we were greeted with interesting trees all around us. There also was a copious growth of a specific plant which I have seen all over the western ghats in Karnataka and Maharashtra. It has leaves which cause itching if rubbed against the skin. I do not know what it is called and need to find it out.

The forest section between KP and Sesha Parcatha has to be one of the best sections of this trek. When you are walking alone here, it feels very peaceful.

On reaching Sesha Parvatha, the clouds cleared for a bit and we were able to get some good views of the valley below us.

We continued the trek further down and decided to take a break at Kallu Mantapa. We also had to refill our water bottles a short distance below this point. I clicked a few pics of the grasslands around me and dense forest visible at a distance from where I was sitting.

We kept walking and made for the forest office at Girigadde. By this time it was already late in the evening and we knew that it would be late in the night by the time we finished the trek. A couple of people in our groups were injured and 3 of us decided to stay back with them. I came across this tiny pond just before we were about to reach the forest office.

Earlier, camping used to be allowed at Girigadde forest office and I have camped there twice. I remember spending most of my time during those times sitting on a bench and admiring the valley all around me. This time, we did not have enough time to do so, but I still went to the bench just to refresh my memories. The camping ground where I had camped on my previous visits was empty.

The views all around this viewpoint are a treat to the eyes.

The trek after this became challenging. The people who were injured found every step difficult to take. We had to help them a bit. We lied to them all throughout the trek, telling them that we were almost at the end of the trek. Finally we reached the exit check post at Kukke a few minutes before 10pm. We got in the bus and reached our homestay after midnight. Just as I was getting out of the trail, I saw this frog sitting in the middle of the path and took a pic.
